Steve Jobs

The man who revolutionized the tech industry through Apple.

Here's a time line of Steve Jobs' life.

  • 1955 - Steve Jobs is born on February 24 in San Francisco, California.
  • 1972 - Jobs drops out of Reed College in Portland, Oregon.
  • 1976 - Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak, and Ronald Wayne found Apple Computer, Inc. in Jobs's garage.
  • 1977 - Apple introduces the Apple II, one of the first highly successful personal computers.
  • 1984 - Apple launches the Macintosh, the first mass-market personal computer with a graphical user interface.
  • 1985 - Jobs leaves Apple following a power struggle and starts NeXT, Inc.
  • 1986 - Jobs acquires The Graphics Group (later known as Pixar) from Lucasfilm.
  • 1996 - NeXT is acquired by Apple, bringing Jobs back to the company.
  • 1997 - Jobs becomes Apple's interim CEO and later assumes the role of permanent CEO.
  • 2001 - Apple introduces the iPod, revolutionizing the music industry.
  • 2004 - Jobs is diagnosed with a rare form of pancreatic cancer.
  • 2007 - Apple unveils the iPhone, changing the mobile phone industry.
  • 2008 - Apple releases the App Store, creating a platform for mobile applications.
  • 2010 - Apple introduces the iPad, establishing a new category of personal devices.
  • 2011 - Steve Jobs resigns as CEO of Apple due to health issues; Tim Cook becomes CEO.
  • 2011 - Steve Jobs passes away on October 5th in Palo Alto, California.
